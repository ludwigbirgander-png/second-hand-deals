# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Personal users and small circles of friends sharing a joint wishlist. They open the app when they're actively hunting something specific — checking whether that Fjällräven backpack dropped in price, or browsing what turned up overnight. Mindset: curious, hopeful, a little like browsing a flea market. Not in a hurry, but not idle either.

## Product Purpose

A personal deal tracker that monitors second-hand listings across Swedish marketplaces (Blocket, Tradera, Vinted, Sellpy, Facebook Marketplace). Users add items to a watchlist, the app scrapes on their behalf, and surfaces the best finds in one place. Success looks like: opening the app and immediately spotting the right item at the right price, then clicking through to buy.

## Brand Personality

Playful · Light · Friendly. Feels handpicked — like a curated vintage shop rather than a comparison engine. Warm without being loud. Considered without being cold. The Are.na school of visual restraint, but with a smile.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboard: no sidebar nav, no metric cards, no gradient banners, no Tailwind template feel.
- Marketplace clone: should not look or feel like Blocket, Tradera, or eBay.
- Dark hacker tool: no terminal aesthetics, no neon-on-black.
- Startup landing page: no hero sections, no gradient text, no animations for their own sake.

## Design Principles

1. **Content earns the frame.** The listings and items are the design. UI chrome should recede, not compete.
2. **Warmth through specificity.** Generic shapes feel cold. Specific choices — a particular type size, an unexpected color moment — make the interface feel considered and human.
3. **Playful, not juvenile.** Friendliness lives in proportion, rhythm, and small delights — not in rounded blobs or cartoon icons.
4. **Shareable confidence.** The app should look good enough to screenshot and send to a friend without embarrassment.
5. **Earn every element.** If it can be removed and the page still makes sense, remove it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Ensure listing images have meaningful alt text. Color should never be the sole carrier of information.
